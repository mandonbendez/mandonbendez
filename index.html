BRANDON MENDEZ

Guitar Performance and Instruction

Brandon Mendez is a California-based guitarist whose abilities encompass a variety of genres and styles, making him one of the most eclectic musicians performing today. From classical to rock and all the way to jazz he is always sure to leave the audience craving more of his delicate yet compelling playing style.

In the summer of 2014, Mendez’s expertise landed him in a two week tour of Spain, performing with a classical guitar quartet amidst the country’s most iconic cities. Prior to this, he had also completed multiple interstate tours of the United States in 2013 and 2014 with a progressive rock group to support his new album release with the band. Every tour date Mendez has played has been greeted with a spirited performance cemented by a dedicated drive for bringing an enjoyable musical showcase to anybody at any time. In addition to the touring, he has also been invited to play live on the radio and television news broadcasts for advertisement of his school’s music programs.

Mendez received his Associates of the Arts degree from Fresno City College in Guitar Performance, graduating with highest honors, the Dana Family Outstanding Performance in Music scholarship and the Friends of the Arts scholarship. He is now a student at California State University Northridge, studying classical guitar performance under the guidance of Steve Thachuk and John Dearman of the Los Angeles Guitar Quartet. Additionally, he was welcomed to join the CSUN Honors Guitar Quartet in the fall of 2015, an ensemble bearing the school’s finest players. Soon to start his Senior year, Brandon also received first prize in the Randy Rhoads Memorial Scholarship guitar competition, as well as earning the Andres Segovia Memorial Scholarship this past May. Furthermore, while attending both of these schools Brandon has been asked to play in masterclasses with some of the greatest guitarists alive today, including; Marcin Dylla, Jason Vieaux, Scott Tennant, Vladimir Gorbach, Lorenzo Micheli, David Tannenbaum, Grisha Goryachev, and lutenist Paul O’Dette.

Brandon is currently working on the preparation of his senior recital, which will be performed during the Spring of 2016, and will be featuring the works of several quintessential composers including; J.S. Bach, J. Rodrigo, J.K. Mertz, and Aaron Jay Kernis. On top of this, he has plans to release a record for his jazz and rock influenced solo project late this 2016.
